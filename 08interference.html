Two-slit interference

In two or three dimensions, the superposition of waves can be more complicated, resulting in interference patterns of constructive or destructive interference. We can see this in real life by dropping two rocks into a still pond, and seeing the checkerboard pattern that results.

No doubt you have seen Young's double-slit experiment in previous classes. Monochromatic waves (i.e. waves with a single frequency) are emitted in phase from two point sources (such as laser light shining through two narrow slits), and projected onto a flat screen a certain distance away.
